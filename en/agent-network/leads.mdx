When agents bring in potential buyers, leads are attributed to the agent who generated them. This page explains how the lead flow works from the developer's perspective.

## How Agent Leads Work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Agent shares project">
    An agent shares a project link or submits a lead through the Agent Cabinet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Lead is created">
    The lead is created in your Gridix account with the agent's attribution.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You review the lead">
    The lead appears in your Leads tab and in the agent's Leads tab within the Agent Network drawer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Commission is tracked">
    If the lead results in a sale, the agent's commission is calculated based on their rate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Viewing Agent Leads

You can see agent-attributed leads in two places:

### From the Leads Tab
Your main Leads tab shows all leads, including those from agents. Agent-attributed leads show the agent's name.

### From the Agent Network Drawer
Open any partner in the Agent Network, go to the **Leads** tab, and see all leads attributed to that specific agent. You can search within this list.

## Lead Attribution

Each lead tracks its source, so you can see:
- Which agent generated the lead
- Which project and apartment the lead is for
- When the lead was submitted
- Current lead status

## Managing Agent Leads

Agent leads follow the same status workflow as regular leads:
- View lead details
- Add internal notes
- Update status
- Send to CRM (if integrated)

The difference is that agent leads are linked to a partner for commission tracking purposes.
